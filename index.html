DIRECTION 01

Cross-Domain Transfer Learning for Seismic Phase Detection

Volcanic-pretrained deep-learning pickers achieve F1 = 0.93 on ~140k induced seismic waveforms — consistently outperforming tectonic-pretrained models despite smaller training sets. The performance advantage stems from superior morphological diversity in volcanic data, and fine-tuning with as little as 10% local waveforms is sufficient to calibrate model confidence in data-limited settings such as enhanced geothermal systems and CO₂ geological storage.

DIRECTION 02

Deep Long-Period Earthquakes as Volcanic Unrest Indicators

Systematic characterisation of deep LP earthquakes across Alaska volcanoes over twelve years shows that LP seismicity consistently outperforms volcano-tectonic earthquakes as a proxy for magmatic flux — closely tracking geodetically-constrained magma influx events at multiple volcanic centres where VT seismicity shows weaker coupling.

03
DIRECTION 03

LP Microseismicity as a Probe of Volcanic Fluid Dynamics and Eruption Potential

ML detection at the Galápagos Islands uncovers a class of LP microseismicity triggered by remote earthquakes and invisible to conventional monitoring. This cryptic fluid activity modulates caldera stability and can facilitate eruptions — establishing a mechanistic link between distal seismic forcing, subsurface fluid redistribution, and volcanic hazard.
