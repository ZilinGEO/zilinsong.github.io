I am an early-career postdoctoral researcher working at the intersection of natural hazard monitoring and geophysical machine learning. I specialise in volcanic seismology characterisation — understanding the physical processes that connect long-period earthquakes to volcanic unrest and eruption potential. A parallel line of work develops transferable AI frameworks for seismic monitoring, with a focus on model generalisation and cross-domain transfer learning across diverse geological settings.

DIRECTION 01

Cross-Domain Transfer Learning for Seismic Phase Detection

We assess the performance of deep-learning seismic pickers using ~140k induced waveforms to understand model generalization. This study bridges the gap between model learning and practical transferability, offering a blueprint for designing efficient monitoring tools in resource-constrained settings.

02
DIRECTION 02

ML-Detected Precursory Seismic Signals at Caldera Volcanoes

Machine-learning detection applied to continuous seismic records reveals a rich catalogue of long-period microseismicity including hidden precursory signals invisible to conventional monitoring. At Sierra Negra Volcano, Galápagos. This seismicity reflects a cryptic phase of fluid activity in the volcanic edifice prior to eruption — demonstrating that ML-enhanced monitoring can uncover seismic signatures of subsurface fluid dynamics and eruption precursors at caldera systems.

03
DIRECTION 03

Volcanic Seismicity Characterisation and Relations to Volcanic Activity

Using template matching on a 12-year seismic dataset, we characterized DLPs and volcano-tectonic earthquakes across Alaska volcanoes. Frequency index differences between the two earthquake types enable effective automatic classification. Most DLPs exhibit non-repeating source mechanisms, and their occurrence shows limited correlation with magma water content, plate motion kinematics, or eruptive activity.
